Organic Farm Plans

We Provide Organic Food For Your Family

Organic farming is an agricultural approach that relies on sustainable and natural methods to cultivate crops and raise livestock. It emphasizes soil health, biodiversity, and the use of organic inputs rather than synthetic chemicals. Here are steps and information about creating an organic farming plan:

100% Organic

Organic farmers rely on natural processes and substances to enhance soil fertility, control pests, and promote plant growth.

Award Winning

We're proud to carry certifications that ensure the integrity of our farming practices, giving you the confidence that you're choosing the very best for your health and the environment.

Site Selection:
1.Choose a location with suitable soil and climate conditions for organic farming. Assess the availability of water resources and ensure proper drainage.
2.Soil Testing and Improvement: Conduct a soil test to determine nutrient levels and pH. Use organic amendments like compost, manure, and cover crops to improve soil fertility and structure.
3.Crop Rotation: Plan a crop rotation system to prevent soil diseases and nutrient depletion. Rotate crops with different nutrient requirements to maintain soil health.
4.Crop Selection: Choose crops that are well-suited to the local climate and soil conditions. Consider diverse crops to promote biodiversity and reduce the risk of pests and diseases.
5.Organic Seeds and Planting: Use organic, non-GMO seeds whenever possible. Follow organic planting methods and spacing guidelines.
6.Weed Management: Implement mechanical methods like hand weeding, mulching, and cover cropping to control weeds. Use natural herbicides if necessary.
7.Pest and Disease Control: Encourage natural predators and beneficial insects. Rotate crops, use companion planting strategies, and employ biological controls to manage pests and diseases.
8.Composting: Develop a composting system to recycle organic matter and kitchen waste. Use compost as a natural fertilizer for crops.
9.Water Management: Implement efficient irrigation systems to conserve water. Use rainwater harvesting methods when possible.
10.Livestock Integration (if applicable): If raising livestock, implement organic practices for feeding and healthcare. Utilize manure as a natural fertilizer.
11.Record Keeping: Maintain detailed records of all farming activities, inputs used, and crop yields. This is essential for organic certification and for analyzing the success of the farming plan.
12.Certification Process: If you wish to sell your produce as certified organic, follow the certification process established by relevant organic certification bodies.
13.Continuous Learning: Stay informed about new organic farming practices, techniques, and research. Attend workshops, conferences, and engage with the organic farming community.
14.Market Planning: Develop a marketing plan for selling organic produce. Identify local markets, organic food stores, and consider direct-to-consumer options.
